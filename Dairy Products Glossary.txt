Cultured dairy products:
Skyr: An Icelandic cultured dairy product, like yogurt but typically thicker and milder in flavour.
Kefir: A fermented milk drink made by adding kefir grains to milk. It has a thinner consistency compared to yogurt and a tangy, effervescent taste.
Labneh: Also known as strained yogurt or yogurt cheese, it's made by draining the whey from yogurt, resulting in a thick, spreadable cheese with a slightly tangy taste.
Labneh Balls: Small balls of labneh, strained yogurt cheese, often marinated in olive oil with herbs and spices.
Buttermilk: Traditionally, it was the liquid left after churning butter, but modern buttermilk is typically cultured milk with a tangy flavour.
Sour Cream: Cream that has been fermented with lactic acid bacteria to give it a thick, creamy texture and a tangy taste.
Clotted Cream: A thick, rich cream with a high-fat content, often associated with British cuisine and commonly served with scones and desserts.
Cottage Cheese: While not necessarily rare, cottage cheese is a specialty dairy product with a unique texture, made by curdling milk and draining the whey.
Crème Fraiche: A thick, cultured cream with a slightly nutty flavour, commonly used as a topping or in sauces.
Aryan: A popular Turkish drink made by mixing yogurt, water, and sometimes salt.
Viili: A traditional Finnish cultured milk product with a gelatinous texture and a mildly tangy taste.
Filmjölk: A Swedish cultured milk product with a texture like kefir but milder in flavour.
Amasi: A fermented milk product commonly consumed in Southern Africa, known for its thick consistency and tangy taste.

Specialty dairy products:
Burrata: An Italian cheese made from mozzarella and cream. It has a soft, creamy centre and a firm outer shell, offering a delicate and rich taste.
Ghee: A clarified butter commonly used in Indian cuisine. It is made by simmering butter until the water evaporates and the milk solids separate, resulting in a nutty and flavourful product.
Mascarpone: An Italian cream cheese with a high-fat content and a smooth, velvety texture. It is often used in desserts like tiramisu.
Halloumi: A Cypriot cheese with a high melting point, making it suitable for grilling or frying. It has a chewy texture and a salty taste.
Quark: A creamy, fresh cheese that originated in Germany. It is like Greek yogurt but has a milder taste and lower fat content.
Paneer: A fresh cheese commonly used in Indian and South Asian cooking. It does not melt when heated and is often cut into cubes for use in curries and other dishes.
Manchego: A Spanish sheep's milk cheese with a nutty flavour and a distinctive herringbone pattern on its rind.
A2 Milk Products: These dairy products come from cows that produce milk containing only the A2 beta-casein protein, believed by some to be easier to digest for people with lactose intolerance.
